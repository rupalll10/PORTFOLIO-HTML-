Tteokbokki(Spicy Rice Cakes)

Tteokbokki is a spicy Korean rice cake that is cylindrical in shape and cooked in spicy sauce, and when we say spicy food, Koreans are pretty serious about that.


INGRIDIENTS:
  • 350g / 12 ounces Korean rice cakes, separated
  • 150g / 5.3 ounces Korean fish cakes, rinsed over hot water & cut into bite size pieces
  • 2 cups Korean soup stock (dried kelp and dried anchovy stock)
  • 3 Tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 1/2 Tbsp raw sugar
  • 1 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp minced garlic
  • 1 tsp gochugaru (Korean chili flakes)
  • 1 tsp roasted sesame seeds
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 stalk green onion, finely chopped

PROCESS:
  1. Unless your rice cakes are soft already, soak them in warm water for 10 mins.
  2. Boil the soup stock in a shallow pot over medium high heat and dissolve the tteokbokki sauce by stirring it with a spatula. Once the seasoned stock is boiling, add the rice cakes, fish cakes and onion. Boil them a further 3 to 5 mins until the rice cakes are fully cooked. Then, to thicken the sauce and to deepen the flavor, simmer it over low heat for a further 2 to 4 mins.
  3. Add the sesame oil, sesame seeds, and green onion then quickly stir. Serve warm.

Gamja-Hot dog(Korean-style French Fries Corn Dog)

Imagine the two of the best snacks in the world, hot dog and french fries combined; the result is the Gamja-Hotdog. This Korean-style French Fries Corn Dog is a fun Korean street food that is made with a hot dog covered in crispy, golden brown potatoes. There are other varieties of Korean corn dogs, like the one with mozzarella, but the Gamja-Hot dog is already a treat for your taste buds..


INGRIDIENTS:
  • 1 cup lukewarm water
  • 2 tablespoons white or brown sugar
  • 1 package dry yeast (about 2 teaspoons)
  • ½ teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1¾ cup all purpose flour
  • 4 uncured beef hot dogs, pat dried
  • 4 disposable wooden chopsticks
  • 1 pound peeled potato, cut into ¼ inch small cubes, rinsed and soaked in cold water
  • ½ cup Korean bread crumbs (ppang-garu) or Japanese panko breadcrumbs/li>
  • 2 tablespoons all purpose wheat flourl
  • corn or vegetable oil for frying
  • sugar, ketchup, and yellow mustard (optional)

PROCESS:
  1. Combine the warm water and sugar in a bowl. Stir well.
  2. Add the active dry yeast and let it sit for a few minutes until the yeast melts.
  3. Add the salt and flour. Mix well with a wooden spoon for about 2 minutes until it turns smooth without any lumps at all.
  4. Cover and let it sit until the volume doubles in size, about 1 hour.
  5. Boil a quart of water in a pan. Drain the potato and add it to the boiling water. Blanch for 2 minutes.
  6. Drain the potato and rinse in cold running water to remove any excess starch.
  7. Put the potato into a bowl and mix with 2 tablespoons flour. Set aside.
  8. Skewer the hot dogs on skewers or wooden chopsticks.
  9. Heat 4 inches of oil in a large frying pan or wok to 320°F- 330°F. The pan should be deep and wide enough to immerse at least one hot dog plus the stick. If you don’t have kitchen thermometer, use a wooden chopstick to dip a bit of dough into the oil. If it bubbles vigorously, it’s ready to fry!
  10. Place the diced potato on your cutting board next to your bread crumbs and your batter, all in a row. Coat a hot dog skewer with the batter, turning the skewer around so it’s fully covered. You can use your hands to shape it a bit, if you need to! Then roll it in the potato bits, and pack them into the batter a bit with your hands, until they stick. Then roll it in the breadcrumbs until it’s completely covered before dropping the battered dog into the hot oil.
  11. Fry for 6 to 7 minutes and use tongs to take it out. Repeat with all of your hot dog skewers.
  12. You can garnish as you like, but Korean street vendors lightly dust each hot dog in sugar, and then drizzle both ketchup and yellow mustard over top.
  13. Serve and eat right away!
